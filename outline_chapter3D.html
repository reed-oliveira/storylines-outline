Chapter 3

Don’t move

There’s no way anything good could come out of following strange things in the dark.

You remain firmly planted in your spot, confident in your decision. Stubborn, even. There’s someone looking for you right now, there has to be. Why wouldn’t there be. You’re someone important, right? You have places to be and things to do. There’s no way you’d just randomly become lost in these woods. Surely it was an accident.

Time passes.

Nothing happens.

You laugh. Feels like it’s been hours, yet the night won’t go. Day won’t come. You’re still exactly where you started, rooted to your spot, your place of comfort. This flat and smooth rock, so accommodating.

This is your spot, now.

Time passes.

Nothing happens.

You pass the time by scooping up dirt in your hand and letting it trickle like sand in an hourglass. It does nothing to quell your boredom. It’s distracting, at least. Soothing, almost. The soil has a great feel to it. Damp, loamy, welcoming. You kick off your shoes and bury your feet in the earth. Nice and cool.

You don’t know if it’s your body adjusting, but you feel less hungry as time goes on. Less thirsty. Less bored. Time stops mattering.

But it still passes. It doesn’t wait.

Not like you have.
