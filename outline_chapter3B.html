Chapter 3

Build some shelter

You finish your drink, refreshed and focused. There’s work to be done, and you’re the only one that can do it.

Of all the things you can build, you settle for a simple hut. Some sticks, leaves and mud are all you need. You can upgrade later, if it comes to that. It doesn’t take long for you to finish your masterpiece.

It looks absolutely terrible and shoddy, but it’ll hold. Time to start a fire as well. It’s getting colder as the night progresses. With some spare branches and dry brush, you strike two stones together, hoping for magic to happen. There’s a spark.

Warmth.

You feel like it’s been hours, but you feel just fine. Hunger pangs strike you occasionally, but the safety of your hut and the comfort of your fire are all that’s occupying your mind. Maybe there’s no way out, but there’s certainly always a way forward.

Mushrooms.

You have no idea how you missed those, but they were growing just out of sight, behind a tree you forgot to check.

Berries.

They grew on the trees! Who would have thought?

A rabbit.

Silent as the night, but curious enough to approach you.

It’s not much, but it’s enough to stave off the hunger. You can always find more. You can always look for more.

Time passes.

Best of a bad situation
