Chapter 2

Wander around

You’ve decided that staying put isn’t the best idea, and set out to see a bit more of the forest.

The satisfying crunch of the earth beneath your every step provides you with some comfort. It’s all you can hear, but it’s a lot better than the eerie silence of the forest. No hooting of owls, no howling of wolves. It’s so quiet you can almost hear your own heartbeat.

Pushing through the thick brush, you ignore the pain from small scratches and the whip of branches against your skin. There’s far more important things to worry about, such as finding an exit, or at least some sign of civilization. You don’t dwell on the thought for long, however.

Something else reaches your ears.

You turn around, realizing you can hear a distinct sound. The ripple of something liquid. Water? Not only are you hungry, but you’re thirsty as well. So thirsty... The idea of fresh water is almost irresistible, and you are drawn to the source of the sound. You continue pushing in a different direction, and soon find a clearing.

A break in the canopy. The moon shines beautifully down upon a still lake, almost like looking at pure silver. Excitedly, you rush over and barely contain the urge to dive, but still take a few hearty gulps. It’s so pure, so cold, so tasty. Nothing beats the sensation of cold water down your throat, the excess trickling past your lips. You drink, and you drink, and you drink. Your thirst is never quenched, but you feel a lot better now. Energized. Ready to take on any challenges.

The only challenge ahead, however, is figuring out where to go from here. You can’t wander forever, and there’s no guarantee you’ll find food at all, or water ever again.

You can keep pushing through the brush to find an exit, or build shelter. There’s certainly no lack of materials.
