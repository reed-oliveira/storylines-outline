Chapter 3

Keep looking

Invigorated by the water, you decide it’s time to find your way out once and for all. Though you still have no recollection of who you are or how you got here, you feel more focused than ever, thinking about the steps you took to get this far.

You lack a compass and there are no landmarks, but you’re certain of the path you took, and following a quick process of elimination, it’s easy to determine which way ‘forward’ is. The brush isn’t going to be any kinder to your tender skin, but getting out of here is worth its weight in blood and scratches.

As you push through, ignoring your pain and exhaustion, it dawns on you that you’ve been in these woods for what feels like many hours, yet the moon is still at its zenith. What a long night... Then again, time tends to pass slower when you’re stressed. It’s never time to go home.

Home? Where even is that? You stop briefly, considering this stray thought. Even if you do make it out of here, you won’t even know where to go. Are you damned to wander forever?

Your body jerks, sending a shiver down your spine. Quickly looking around, you find no threats. It must have been your imagination... It’s time to keep pushing.

Nothing but trees and brush, branches and moss. Why is it so bright, all of a sudden? When did the sun come up? You look up, it didn’t. It’s still night time. What a strange sensation.

You look at a bizarrely-shaped tree some twenty odd feet away from you. It tells you the time. 2:34 PM. What a courteous tree, telling you the time. Wait. How can a tree tell you the time? There’s nothing there. Nothing but a sea of trees. You keep pushing through.

A sudden, shrill noise almost makes you grit your teeth in discomfort. It’s coming at you. It’s coming right for you. You can’t tell where it’s coming from, but it’s definitely coming your way. Your entire body trembles. There’s something hunting you. It’s right behind you. You turn around...

Out of the woods
