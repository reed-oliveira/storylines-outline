Chapter 2

Stay where you are

Maybe there’s people looking for you right now. Staying optimistic in a situation like this may seem difficult, but the rational part of your mind tells you it’ll be okay. There’s no way of telling how big these woods are, and wandering aimlessly would just render any search efforts completely null.

You remain seated on the flat, smooth rock that served you so well thus far. It’s oddly comfortable, almost like it was made for you. There’s not much to do but take in the sights, and twiddle your thumbs. It’s so quiet you can almost hear your own heartbeat.

If only there was some way you could tell people where you are. Would a phone even work out here? The thought becomes irrelevant, as you recall the only possessions you have are the clothes on your back.

As your mind wanders, you notice something peculiar in the distance.

Far beyond a thick section of brush and trees, you see a flicker of violet and red. Dancing lights, like a mirage on a hot day. You rub your eyes to make sure you’re not hallucinating, but the lights seem very real. It’s almost as if they’re calling out to you... They could be signal flares, but none like any you have ever seen.

A trap, perhaps? You never know what’s lurking in the dark.

Your body jerks back by instinct as the lights draw suddenly closer. They’re still a ways from you, but a sudden movement like that would put anyone on edge. Sweat forms on your brow. You’re almost compelled to take a closer look, but fear keeps you firmly planted where you are.

Bravery can be as much of a virtue as it can be a flaw.

Follow the lights
Don’t move
