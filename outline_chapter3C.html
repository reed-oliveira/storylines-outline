Chapter 3

Follow the lights

This is such a bad idea.

You’ve watched horror movies before. Investigating things that are out of place gets you killed. Wandering off the beaten path gets you killed. You can’t wander much more than this, you suppose, so it ends up being a “why the hell not” sort of situation. Imagine, for a moment, that following the lights will net any kind of positive outcome.

You could say it did.

Approaching the strange blur of red and violet, you finally see it for what it is. Two small orbs of light, no bigger than a fist, fluttering and flying through the air. They dance around you excitedly, not making a sound. You don’t get to enjoy their company for long, as they take off through the trees.

You think to yell at them to wait, but actions speak louder than words. It’s time to run and push through the brush. Your gut tells you to follow the lights, foolish as that sounds, and you do just that. They’re trying to show you something, or lure you into an even worse predicament. Beats staying in one place, really. Beats staying in these woods.

The forest becomes lighter and lighter. The dark silhouettes of trees give rise to tiny details in the bark as light shines on them. Hope surges within you. You’re getting somewhere. The lights keep flying, and you follow. The canopy becomes thinner and thinner.

There’s something in the distance.

Light.
